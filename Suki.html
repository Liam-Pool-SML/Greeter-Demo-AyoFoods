SM Research

The Soul Machines research division is led by our Chief Science Officer and co-founder, Mark Sagar. Our research is focused on humanizing AI to bring the goodness of human-machine collaborations to the world. 

 

Our research centers around the Digital Brain we created to make Digital People more human.  The Digital Brain is the result of advanced research into brain models, virtual neurotransmitters, physical embodiment, and motor control systems. It all started with the simulation of a brain and a baby, called Baby X. 

 

Over seven years ago, Soul Machines pioneered research into progressing human-machine collaboration with Baby X. By combining models of physiology, cognition and emotion with advanced lifelike CGI, we set out to create a new form of biologically inspired AI.

 

Baby X was our first developmental prototype designed as both a stand-alone research project and as an expandable base to feed into commercial computer agents. She enables us to explore human cooperation with machines and the foundations for creating a digital consciousness.

 

Although Baby X was designed for research and has allowed Soul Machines to explore the models of human behavior, she was also the genesis of our approach to our autonomously animated AI avatars, now available in DDNA Studio, democratizing the creation of Digital People at scale.

Baby X provides a foundation from which we learn, experiment, and continue to develop the world’s first end-to-end solution for dynamically creating, teaching, managing, and deploying Digital People.

 

Baby X is our Soul Machines origin story, the focus of our ongoing pioneering research, and we are incredibly proud of her.
